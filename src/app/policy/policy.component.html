Content Guidelines
At Edunaut, we’re dedicated to helping learners navigate through the world of online courses. We're building a space that facilitates the discovery of learning opportunities which can be charted into pathways. Our Content Policies intend to provide clarity on interactions with all of Edunaut’s features and services.

To help us maintain Edunaut’s integrity, please ensure that your content is -

  • Friendly

    • Our community is diverse and passionate. While participating, it’s important to be respectful of opposing or differing opinions, beliefs, and conclusions. We value critical dialogue, as long as it is civil and considerate. Edunaut does not tolerate content that attacks or disparages an individual or group based on their race, gender, religion, nationality, ethnicity, political group, sexual orientation or another similar characteristic, or content that is illegal, obscene, threatening, defamatory, or otherwise injurious to third parties.

  • Suitable for all audiences

    • We are focused on creating a comfortable learning space and do not tolerate sexual or suggestive content involving minors or endorsing gratuitous violence to oneself and others.

  • Original

    • Any content plagiarized from other websites, reviewers, emails, or printed materials will be removed. Content that is copied or paraphrased from other sources should be clearly indicated (eg: by formatting using quotes) and the source should be explicitly attributed using a link or citation.

  • Discreet

    • We expect learners to be respectful of privacy. Edunaut is not the forum for sharing private information, financial or otherwise. You commit this kind of invasion of privacy by publishing private facts about an individual or entity, the publication of which would be offensive to a reasonable person or said entity. You may not use a false e-mail address, impersonate any person or entity, or otherwise mislead as to the origin of content. Scammers are not welcome.

  • Non-commercial

    • We prohibit spam and self-promotional content whose purpose appears to be to direct traffic to external commercial sites while providing little value to the Edunaut Community. This includes, but is not restricted to, software viruses, political campaigning, commercial solicitation, chain letters, mass mailings, or any form of "spam" or unsolicited commercial electronic messages.

  • Relevant

    • Content needs to provide value to the Edunaut Community. Although humor is welcome if it is helpful to sincerely interested learners, off-topic contributions are not tolerated.

  • Accurate

    • We will remove factual claims and/or primary conclusions that are substantially incorrect, as validated by neutral, high quality sources.

  • Clearly Written

    • Correct formatting, spelling, punctuation, grammar and capitalization is appreciated.

Please keep in mind the spirit in which these guidelines were written, and know that looking for loopholes is a waste of time. We appreciate all that you contribute to the Edunaut community. Thanks for being a part of it!
Edunaut reserves the right to, in its sole discretion, monitor, reject and/or remove any User Content at any time.

Review Guidelines
Our Philosophy
Edunaut is for expressing your honest opinions about online courses. Don't be afraid to say what you think! We welcome your spirited contribution, as it helps other learners on Edunaut understand what a course is really about, and decide whether or not they want to pursue it.

Here’s what we appreciate in reviews-

  • Creativity

    • Feel free to use the online course as inspiration for writing a personal expression. As long as they don't go against our guidelines in other ways, these reviews are welcome and encouraged!

  • Descriptions

    • Pure summaries of course content are welcome.

  • Harshly critical statements

    • That apply to the online course itself. We will always welcome and encourage honest opinions such as “This guy can’t teach for nuts” or “This course is absolutely worthless”.

We believe that Edunaut members should see the best, most relevant and thought provoking reviews (positive and negative) when they visit any course page. Our aim is to show members these reviews, while simultaneously not showing reviews that we think are inappropriate or of insufficient quality.

We value that our learners trust us with their perspectives, and take our stewardship of storing your reviews seriously. We promise to always store your reviews on your profile and will never delete or modify them – except for certain extreme situations, which are described below. Your thoughts and words are yours, and we promise you we will always respect that.

In addition to our Content Policy, here are some specific things we do not tolerate in the context of Reviews-

  • Reviews of the course instructor

    • Mentioning the instructor in the context of a review is acceptable, but reviews that are predominantly about an instructor’s behavior outside the context of the online course are considered off-topic and irrelevant and will be deleted. We believe in separating the art from the artist.

  • Reviews that attack other reviewers

    • Statements like "Other reviews have said this course is wonderful, but I disagree" are fine, we do not tolerate reviews whose primary purpose is to mock or harass Edunaut member(s).

  • Abuse of our ratings system

    • We do not tolerate artificial inflation/deflation techniques. We will remove those ratings we determine to be abusive.

Edunaut reserves the right to remove a review at any time for any reason. It is at our sole discretion and no one else's, that we decide when a review is against our guidelines.

Please keep in mind the spirit in which these guidelines were written, and know that looking for loopholes is a waste of time.

Thanks,
Team Edunaut

Please note that the reviews posted on Edunaut are individual and subjective opinions. The opinions expressed in reviews are those of Edunaut members and not of Edunaut LLP. We do not endorse any of the opinions expressed by reviewers.
